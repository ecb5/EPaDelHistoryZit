    Subsection 9.1 Meeting Sites

    Chapter 8 indicates that by the year 2000, the concept of a Spring Meeting was well established, having occurred for 25 consecutive years. The Centennial Era is characterized by the gradual erosion of the idea that Fall Meetings and Spring Meetings are of different flavors.
    The modern era is characterized by a shift toward broader participation and inclusivity, so we pause here to examine how the meeting sites in this era reflect this shift. The sites of the combined 146 meetings, including the 97 annual fall meetings, the 43 annual spring meetings, the special meetings held in April and October 1975, and the special meeting in October 1990. In the first 75 years of its history, 35 separate institutions hosted section meetings. Table 9.1.1 lists all institutions that have hosted meetings in the years since 2001, denoting with * those sites who hosted for the first time during this period.
    Table 9.1.2 lists the 43 different institutions that have hosted at least one of the section’s 146 meetings from the organizational meeting in 1926 through the fall meeting in the year 2022. The final column in Table 9.1.2 classifies each institution into one of five categories: D (doctoral university), S (belongs to the State System of Higher Education), F (other four-year school that does not offer doctoral programs), B (branch campus), and CC (Community College). Table 9.1.3 summarizes the hosting institutions for the 146 meetings according to this classification. The second column gives the number of different institutions in each category. The final column indicates that the section’s meetings were held primarily at four-year schools or doctoral universities (77% altogether). In the first 75 years (1926-2000) of section history, the number of meetings at institutions from category F (45 out of 75) was close to the number from category D (40 out of 75), but the gap widened in the ensuing years. In the new millennium, almost half of all section meetings (21 of 43) were at category F institutions, and the number of meetings at category D institutions (7 out of 43) fell below the number at state system universities (9 of 43) in that time span.
    Note that the COVID-19 pandemic led to the cancellation of the Spring 2020 section meeting as well as to the necessity to invent a virtual meeting category (V) that applied to four meetings from Fall 2020 through Spring 2022.
