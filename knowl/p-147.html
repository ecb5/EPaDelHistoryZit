It is this proclivity toward applied mathematics that makes Reynolds seem like an improbable candidate to play such a vital role in founding the Philadelphia Section of the MAA 3 . Yet the very notion of such a section was his alone. In this sense the role that Reynolds played in the founding of the Philadelphia section is reminiscent of the roles played by the first five presidents of the AMS 4 , none of whom was a pure mathematician.
