James Oliver Brooks was born in Evanston, Illinois. After obtaining his A.B. from Oberlin College in 1952 he entered the graduate program at the University of Michigan, receiving an M.A. the following year and a Ph.D. in 1964. His dissertation on the theory of representation of modules was written under Donald Higman. While completing his graduate studies, Brooks accepted an assistant professorship at Haverford College, which he held from 1959 until 1964. He then moved to nearby Villanova University, where he taught until 1993. Brooks served the department as acting chair for one year, followed by an eight-year stint as chair, 1969-1977.
