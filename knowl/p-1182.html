Robert Alan Styer
Ph.D. Adviser: Harold Stark
Dissertation Title: Hecke Theory over Complex Quadratic Fields
graduated summa cum laude with a degree in mathematics and physics from the University of Pennsylvania, and he earned his PhD from M.I.T. with a dissertation in analytic number theory under Harold Stark. In 1983, after a stint as the Lawton Lecturer at Temple University, Styer joined the faculty of Villanova University, where he taught a wide range of courses and held many positions in university leadership, including Chair of the Department of Mathematics Sciences (later the Department of Mathematics and Statistics) (1997-2003), Chair of the University Senate (2008-2014). He has been the Director of the Comprehensive Science program since 2011 and expects to remain in that position until about 2026.
