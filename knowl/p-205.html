Recall that F. M. Weida spoke at the annual AMS 1 -MAA 2  meeting at Penn in December 1926. His invited lecture to open the 1928 annual meeting of the Philadelphia Section was on numerical analysis, the only time this topic was addressed in the five-year period. He began the lecture by declaring, “The theory of errors is a branch of mathematics which belongs to practical analysis in applied mathematics.” We pause to profile Weida before analyzing other lectures.
