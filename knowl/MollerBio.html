Trisha Moller
Advisor: Garth Timothy Isaak
Dissertation Title: \(t\)-Split Interval Orders
was born in Rahway, NJ, and for most of her youth, she aspired to become an artist. After tutoring her best friend in high school, mathematics won her over, but she has maintained love for both mathematics and art for her whole career. Trisha earned a BS in Mathematics and Secondary Education from Trenton State College (now The College of NJ) in 1997, and then worked for AT&T for a year before enrolling in the graduate program at Lehigh University. In 2004 Trisha earned her PhD in Mathematics from Lehigh University with a dissertation in graph theory under the guidance of Garth Isaak. It was in graduate school at Lehigh University that Trisha met and married fellow mathematician, Mike Fraboni. After a start on the tenure track at DeSales University, Trisha transitioned to full-time mom and part-time professor with the birth of her two sons. Trisha has held many positions on the EPADEL executive committee such as Section NExT coordinator, chair of the Committee on the Session for New Colleagues, Student Speaker Coordinator, Public Information Officer, and organizer of the 2005 EPADEL Student Career Conference. Trisha is a 2004 MAA Project NExT fellow, and in 2011, she earned the AAUW Bethlehem Emerging Star Award. In 2021, the Calypso Elementary Dr. Trisha Moller Leadership Award was established in her honor.
